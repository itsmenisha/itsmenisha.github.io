The Struggle of Finding My Path: A Journey of Learning and Growth


It's been a rollercoaster of a journey, one full of learning, challenges, and constant reevaluation. Not that I am at the end; it is still going on, even as I write this. Looking back, I often found myself standing at the crossroads of different interests and skills, unsure of what direction to take next.

I started my journey with a passion for Machine Learning (ML), eager to dive into this world of endless possibilities. I have done a lot of projects during that time. I started the Angle Yu 100 Days of Code challenge but stopped halfway through. Along the way, however, I discovered a wealth of additional skills that have shaped my experience but also left me feeling somewhat torn. SEO, content writing, and even working on a blog were unexpected but valuable additions to my skill set. I had no idea that I'd find myself writing content, learning how to optimize for search engines, and experimenting with various writing styles. But that’s the beauty of this journey—it’s not linear, and sometimes the unexpected paths teach you more than you could have imagined. It took continuously sitting in front of my laptop thinking, "What next? What more can I do? Am I sure I will enjoy doing this every day?"—questions whose answer I still don't know.

Then came Cinemarga, my first real project. It was a rollercoaster ride, with moments of excitement and frustration. I poured my heart into it, facing the challenges of front-end development, and when it was over, I was left with a treasure trove of lessons. I became proficient in front-end technologies—HTML, CSS, and JavaScript—but also found myself asking, "Now what?" I didn’t know what to do with all this front-end knowledge I had gained. The excitement of building something tangible was quickly replaced by uncertainty about my next steps.

Despite the confusion, one thing was clear: Machine Learning still called to me. It had been my original passion, and I wasn’t ready to give up on it. I’ve been learning Python, honing my skills in data science, and exploring different ML models. But as I delve deeper, I realize that ML itself is vast, with so many directions to explore. There’s supervised learning, unsupervised learning, deep learning, reinforcement learning, and so much more. Each subfield has its own opportunities and challenges, and I find myself wondering: which one should I focus on?
After a break of a year, in these past 2 months, I’ve restarted and completed a Python course and created about 4-5 new projects that got me started in Python again. Will I stick to this journey?


The Struggle: What’s the Next Step?

The options are overwhelming, and I’m constantly torn between diving deeper into front-end development, exploring full-stack development and starting MERN or whatever is in fashion, or sticking with my ML aspirations. The more I learn, the more questions I have. Should I build more real-world applications in front-end development? Should I continue with Python and focus on data science? Or should I refine my ML skills and specialize in a particular subfield? I mean, pure ML is still harder than other prospects. There is also a data mining course in our last semester of bachelors, and maybe that could be the next step, but it all feels so real, like I should actively make the best decision for me right now to mature this process.

Each step I take is a step toward learning, but it also feels like a step away from something else. It’s a bit like standing at a crossroads, not sure which direction to go, but knowing that every path will teach me something valuable. The key is figuring out how to combine my love for development and my interest in ML in a way that makes sense for me.


A Journey of Constant Growth

The beauty of all this is that I’ve been able to explore. Over the years, I’ve picked up more than I ever expected SEO, content writing, front-end development, and machine learning. And while it’s been a struggle at times, I know this journey has been invaluable. I’ve learned that sometimes the struggle is part of the process. The uncertainty, the questioning, and even the frustration of not knowing what comes next, it’s all part of the journey.

Ultimately, I’m beginning to realize that I don’t have to choose just one path. Web development, ML, data science—all of these things can coexist. What matters is finding a way to combine them in a way that aligns with my passions and goals. And while the next right step might not be immediately clear, I know that the experiences I’ve gained so far will guide me toward the right decision. Maybe I will build a company that fits all the things I have learned (half-baked as they may be).

For now, I’ll continue to learn, experiment, and explore. Who knows where this journey will take me? But one thing is for sure: I’ll be ready for whatever comes next.
Human mind is surely a wonderful thing, and sitting idly even now, writing this, I can truly imagine 250 more things I could do. It surely is a real-life "Patha Poster Nikla Hero" situation.
